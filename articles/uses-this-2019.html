Uses This

Who are you, and what do you do?

I’m Brian, and I am a product manager at Know Me for both their apps. I also co-host the Simple Beep podcast with Ed Cormany, and ride skateboards long distances for fun.

What hardware do you use?

For work-work, I use a retina MacBook Air. It replaced a very similar machine, the MacBook Pro without a Touch Bar, which went to AppleCare way too many times for keyboard, logic board, and other failures. It sits on a Twelve South Curve stand, on an Autonomous SmartDesk 2, with a Magic Keyboard and Mouse. When recording audio, I speak into a Shure PG42 microphone and listen through B&O earbuds.

My phone is the iPhone XR. I’m fully adapted to the full-screen lifestyle, and wouldn’t want to go back to Touch ID and the home button. But this phone is tall and heavy. When actively using it, it’s fantastic. When carrying it around until I can use it next, it’s a drag. I look forward to seeing how frequently I can use the Light Phone 2 instead when it comes out this summer.

My skateboard equipment has changed over time. Right now I’m fortunate to have a custom 36” drop deck from Subsonic, mounted on 180mm Paris trucks and 75mm Orangatang wheels. I have a basic Bern helmet, but I’ve backed the Park & Diamond helmet on Indiegogo. The ability to compress it into a water bottle-sized case is really appealing for travel, and barring any project delays or serious safety concerns, I’m looking forward to switching to that this summer.

I used to (cautiously) wear the stock wired EarPods while skating or running for exercise, but my sweat destroyed them. Then I bought some sweatproof wired Yurbuds and they were just okay. When the second generation AirPods came out, I bought them and started using a sweatband. Truly wireless headphones are excellent for exercising outdoors.

And what software?

Here we go. I try to use as little software to accomplish as much as possible, though I have strong software opinions for someone who claims to be so basic.

On macOS, I do a whole lot in Safari, including Gmail. Chrome feels more bloated, whether or not that’s true anymore. I write everything from code to prose to notes in TextEdit, configured to create new documents in plain text rather than the default rich text. For any graphics work, I use Pixelmator Pro. I used regular Pixelmator for a long time and the upgrade was well worth it. And I use Transmit to move files around online (including my Dropbox account, which I only use to share audio with Ed now).

For the podcast, I capture recordings using Ecamm’s Skype recorder and QuickTime Player locally. I edit in GarageBand using Jason Snell’s recommended compressor settings, run post-processing through Levelator (while I still can), and add chapters in Forecast.

On iOS, I also do a whole lot in Safari, including Gmail. I love Castro. It fits my mental model for managing and queuing podcast episodes perfectly, and now it has all the extra effects that are table stakes for a podcast app. I’ve used Instapaper since before I had an iPhone. And the native app for my side project Pastecard, which feels like a cheat because it’s not in the App Store, and ideally its progressive web app would be good enough.

On both, I use Tweetbot for Twitter and News Explorer for RSS. Concerning Twitter, it may be inevitable that I have to switch back to first-party experiences soon. It’s the only way to vote on Le Batard Show polls, and they took a big step toward sanity when they made the “turn off algorithmic timeline” setting actually remove the other timeline junk. On the other hand, they’re still one of those companies that issue 100MB+ updates every week with purposefully vague release notes, and I really dislike that. Concerning RSS, I’m happy with News Explorer, but will almost certainly switch to the upcoming NetNewsWire rewrite if and when it has an iOS app that syncs over CloudKit. That’s what News Explorer does and I appreciate not having to use a separate syncing service from the reader app.

There’s also “behind the scenes software” like extensions. On both macOS and iOS, I use 1Blocker (X) and really can’t go back to the wild, untamed Internet covered in ads anymore. I also use 1Password on both, the buy-once model, and enjoy the system integration in iOS 12. Castle has been pretty good at blocking the robocalls that spoof similar numbers to mine. And Backblaze has my laptop’s internal hard drive as well as my big external drive backed up safely for peace of mind.

Is skateboarding software, like, the clothes I wear? If so, that’s a good wicking t-shirt and lightweight athletic shorts with pockets. I did a couple trips using Keen sandals as my footwear, and now I’ve finally switched to a more sane choice of mesh Nike SB shoes and Dri-Fit socks.

What would be your dream setup?

I loved the second-generation MacBook Air and really enjoy the latest model. I think it could only really be improved by a TrueTone P3 gamut display running at the equivalent of 1440x900 retina, instead of 1280x800. Unimaginably long battery life and no threat of keyboard failure would be nice as well.

My dream phone would be an X-style 5 or 4 with the guts of the XR: same camera and flash (no bump?), battery capacity, A12, speakers, the works! All the benefits of the XR but much smaller, with a notched edge-to-edge (TrueTone, wide-gamut, etc.) display.

If Castro adds a standalone watch app, I’ll immediately buy the base model Series 4 watch. Combining that with AirPods and being able to leave the phone behind sounds like heaven.
